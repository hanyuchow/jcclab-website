Biochemistry

Teaching second-year undergraduate students in the second semester. The course focuses on the components of living organisms, including the structure and function of biomacromolecules such as proteins, carbohydrates, lipids, and nucleic acids, as well as energy transformation and substance metabolism.

Molecular Biology

Teaching third-year undergraduate students over an academic year. The course explores gene expression, the structure and function of DNA and RNA, and regulatory mechanisms at the molecular level, integrating the latest related technologies and trends.

Cancer Drug Resistance

Investigating the mechanisms of cancer cell resistance to chemotherapy drugs and developing new strategies to overcome drug resistance, thereby enhancing therapeutic efficacy.

Verification and Development of Chinese Herbal Medicine Efficacy

Using modern scientific methods to verify the efficacy of traditional Chinese medicine components and developing herbal formulations with potential therapeutic value.

Protein Purification and Identification

Purifying target proteins through advanced techniques and conducting structural and functional analyses to provide foundational data for biological research.

MicroRNA research

Examining the role of microRNAs in gene regulation and disease progression, particularly their impact on cancer and immune diseases.

Disease Animal Model

Developing disease-related animal models, such as acute lung injury, urinary tract infection, osteoporosis, or stroke, to simulate human disease processes for studying pathophysiological mechanisms and drug development.

Bioinformatics

Applying bioinformatics tools to analyze biological big data, including genomic, transcriptomic, and proteomic information, to support precision medicine, drug discovery, and the design of personalized treatment strategies.
